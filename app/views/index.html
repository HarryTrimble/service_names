Why service names are important

What to call a service is the most important conversation a service team can have.

Do it well, you'll make your service:

  • easier to find
  • clearer to understand
  • have less calls from users asking 'how do I...?'

How to name your service

There are principles you can follow to name your service.

Good service names:

  • come from user research
  • describe the problem it solves for a user
  • describe a task, not a technology
  • are verbs, not nouns
  • use the words users use
  • don't have government names in them
  • don't change with policy or technology

Examples

Examples of good service names:

  • Register to vote
  • Help with court fees
  • Renew passport
  • Find an apprenticeship

Measure performance

There are metrics that indicate how a service name is performing:

  • increased page views from organic search
  • click through rate to a 'Start' button
  • external search volumes between old and new name
  • reduce number of on page searches about the service
  • number of users calling to ask "How do I...?"
